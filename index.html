Milestones in Computer History

Tracing the Evolution of Digital Age

Computer technology has grown through many important milestones. Each one has helped shape the world we live in today

Timeline of Computer History

4000 BCE

Early Counting Devices (Pre-Computer Era)

The Abacus

Created by the Chinese, is often regarded as the first computing device. It consisted of beads strung on rods and was used to perform simple arithmetic operations like addition and subtraction.

Enabled faster arithmetic compared to manual counting. It improved trade and early record-keeping efficiency.

1617

Napier's Bones

Invented by John Napier, Napier's Bones were a set of ivory rods engraved with numbers, designed to assist with multiplication and division. This invention also introduced the concept of the decimal point, a crucial development in simplifying calculations.

Simplified multiplication and division for scholars. It reduced calculation time and error rates in mathematics.

1642-1644

Mechanical Calculators (17th-19th Century)

Pascaline

French mathematician Blaise Pascal developed the Pascaline, the first mechanical calculator capable of performing addition and subtraction. It used gears and wheels to calculate, and its purpose was to help Pascalâ€™s father, a tax collector, with his work.

Automated addition and subtraction through gears. It improved speed and accuracy for financial tasks.

1673

Stepped Reckoner

German philosopher and mathematician Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz improved Pascal's design, developing the Stepped Reckoner. It was capable of performing addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division, and it used fluted drums instead of gears.

Automated addition and subtraction through gears. It improved speed and accuracy for financial tasks.
